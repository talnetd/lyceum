## Module summary
Three pillars: **effective communication**, **ethical responsibility**, and **continuous professional development**. Aimed at the broader skills employers expect of computer scientists beyond technical expertise — written/verbal/visual communication, ethical and legal awareness, teamwork, project management, and career planning.

## Module structure (5 areas)
1. Foundations of professional practice in computer science
2. Communication skills — written, verbal, and visual
3. Ethical, legal, and sustainability considerations
4. Teamwork and project management
5. Career planning and lifelong learning

## Learning outcomes
On successful completion you should be able to:
1. Communicate technical and non-technical concepts clearly via written, verbal, and visual mediums.
2. Explain key laws, regulations, and ethical codes for computing professionals and apply them to scenarios.
3. Recognise sustainability impacts (e-waste, energy use) and propose ethically-aligned improvements.
4. Evaluate systems and data for bias and accessibility issues; recommend inclusive solutions.
5. Apply risk management to identify, analyse, and mitigate risks in a computing project.
6. Select appropriate project management methods/tools for team collaboration.
7. Develop and follow a plan for continuous skill development and professional advancement.
