# Presentation Zen

A design philosophy for presentations developed by **Garr Reynolds** (book: *Presentation Zen*). It is a deliberate counterweight to text-heavy, bullet-point-driven slide decks.

## Three foundational values
- **Simplicity** — strip away everything non-essential.
- **Restraint** — fewer elements, more impact.
- **Naturalness** — visuals support the speaker, never overshadow them.

## Five operational principles
1. Keep it simple — one main idea per slide.
2. Use high-quality, relevant images (no cliché stock).
3. Minimize text — apply the [[six-by-six-rule]].
4. Use contrast to highlight what matters.
5. Tell a story — slides as chapters.

## Why it works
Aligns with [[visual-memory-retention]]: relevant visuals dramatically increase what an audience retains. Slides that compete with the speaker for attention (dense text, busy animations) destroy the effect.

## When this applies
Conference talks, client pitches, teaching, exam-style presentations, technical demos — anywhere a presenter is the primary information channel and slides are support.
