# Visual Memory Retention

People retain **~65%** of information when it is presented with a **relevant** visual, compared to only **~10%** without one. The same content lands roughly **6.5× better** when paired with an image, diagram, or chart that reinforces the message.

This is the empirical foundation for the case that visual design in presentations is not cosmetic, it is load-bearing.

## Caveats
- "Relevant" is doing serious work. **Irrelevant visuals can reduce retention** by splitting attention between the speaker and the slide.
- The 65 / 10 split is the figure cited in CM2045; exact percentages vary by study. Treat as an order-of-magnitude effect, not a precise constant.
- Effects are stronger for unfamiliar material than for already-known content.

## Application
- Justifies the [[presentation-zen]] approach of investing in a few high-quality, relevant visuals rather than many decorative ones.
- Reinforces why the [[six-by-six-rule]] matters: text-dense slides crowd out the visual channel.
