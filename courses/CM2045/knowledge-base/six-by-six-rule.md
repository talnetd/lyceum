# Six-by-Six Rule

A heuristic for slide text density: **at most 6 words per line, at most 6 lines per slide**.

Treat it as an **upper bound, not a target** — "less is more" still applies. The point is to force distillation. If you need more text than that to make a slide work, the content probably belongs in speaker notes or split across multiple slides.

## Why it works
- Dense slides cause the audience to **read instead of listen**, fragmenting attention.
- Distilling content forces the speaker to identify what is genuinely essential.
- Aligns with the simplicity pillar of [[presentation-zen]].

## When to break it
Rare cases where omitting information would be misleading or unsafe — for example, dense reference slides in medical, legal, or compliance contexts. These should be the exception.

## Alternatives to text
When you'd exceed the rule, prefer:
- A chart or diagram
- An icon or symbol
- Splitting across two slides
- Moving detail into speaker notes
