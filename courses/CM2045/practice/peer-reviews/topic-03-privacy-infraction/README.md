# Peer Review: Real-World Privacy Infraction Analysis

> CM2045 · Topic 3 (Legal & Ethical Foundations) · peer-review activity (formative)
> Ties to notes: [`../../../notes/03-legal-ethical-foundations/`](../../../notes/03-legal-ethical-foundations/03-legal-ethical-foundations.md)

## Task
Critically analyse a real-world privacy infraction, then give constructive feedback on a peer's analysis of a similar case. Two deliverables:

1. **My analysis** (500-700 words) of one chosen case: `analysis.md`
2. **Peer feedback** on three peers' submissions: `peer-feedback.md`

## Case options
- Cambridge Analytica scandal (2018), Facebook data harvested for political ads without consent
- Equifax data breach (2017), 147M people exposed via an unpatched known vulnerability
- Clearview AI controversy (2020), billions of images scraped to build a facial-recognition database
- Marriott / Starwood breach (2018), ~500M guest records exposed
- Yahoo! breaches (2013 & 2014), all ~3B accounts compromised

## What the analysis must cover
| Aspect | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Nature of the infraction | Which privacy principles were violated (collection, security, retention, usage)? |
| Impact | Who was affected and how? Consequences for individuals and the organisation? |
| Legal & ethical implications | Laws/regulations broken? Ethical considerations raised? |
| Lessons learned | How to prevent recurrence? What should individuals and orgs do? |

## How it is assessed (also the lens for reviewing peers)
- **Accuracy & completeness**, facts correct, no gaps or errors
- **Clarity & organisation**, clear, concise, easy to follow
- **Depth of analysis**, goes beyond surface into legal/ethical implications
- **Critical thinking**, insightful, not just descriptive
- **Constructive feedback**, specific strengths + concrete improvements
